About Us

TEAM AHARYA is an arts education organization established on 22nd September 2019,based in Kalady,Ernakulam. A group of young energetic artists are the nurturers of Aharya.When everyone turns to performances, Aharya took its first step to introduce Dance and Nritta - Natya Grandhas to the common people. Various programs are also organized as a part of this concept. This organization takes care to find people who are interested in art forms, economically backward areas and ensure good training in Dance and Music. Team Aharya is moving forward with many missions,conducting performances and presentations of major indian classical dances, Bharathanatyam, Mohiniyattam, Kuchippudi, Kathakali, Odissi, Kathak, Manipuri, Sattriya, and other traditional art forms with aiming to reach all over the world.

Management

Shri Praveen Kaimadathil, who has been working in various arts and social fields of the society for 13 years. In 2013 with the aim of bringing Bharatanatyam to the common people, he established a dance school called Cochin Natyashala in Kaladi, Adi Shankaran's hometown, Kerala. After 6 years, he established the Team Aharya, a group of young artists interested in various art forms. He completed Bachelor's and Master's degree in Bharatanatyam from Sri Shankaracharya Sanskrit University, while simultaneously deal with subjects such as dance, drama and music.In 2017, he was awarded the research fellowship of the Kerala Culture Department and the Centre for Cultural Resources and Training (CCRT) from government of India in 2018.

Praveen Kaimadathil

Founder,CEO

Sreelakshmi Warrier is a celebrated maestro of the Kuchipudi dance form, renowned for her mesmerizing performances that transport audiences to a different world.Armed with a diploma in both Bharatanatyam and Kuchipudi, Sreelakshmi Warrier went on to earn a prestigious MA from Hyderabad Central University, cementing her position as a force to be reckoned with in the world of Indian classical dance.With a wealth of experience under her belt, Sreelakshmi Warrier has taken on the role of mentor at the Aharya School of Performing Arts and Sreepadam Nruthavidhyalayam, where she inspires young dancers to explore the limitless possibilities of this ancient art form.
